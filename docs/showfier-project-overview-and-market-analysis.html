Scenario Simulations

🔥
Scenario A: "Slow Burn"
Organic growth only, no marketing budget

50 users at M6, 200 at M12, 500 at M24. MRR reaches $4,400 by month 24. ARR ~$53K.

Verdict: Viable as a side project with parallel income. Not viable as sole income. Break-even on infrastructure at month 3–4.

🚀
Scenario B: "Viral Hit"
Major engineer tweets, PSW thread goes viral

500 users month 1, 2,000 by month 6. MRR hits $18K. Infrastructure handles fine. Support burden: 50 tickets/week at 500 users = 12.5 hrs/week solo — unsustainable at 2,000.

Verdict: Revenue is real ($216K ARR). Support will bury you before you enjoy it. Pre-build FAQ and hire part-time support at month 2–3.

⚠️
Scenario C: "Manufacturer Threat"
Yamaha adds DiGiCo import in firmware

Removes ~10–15% of use cases (one pair of N×(N-1)). Only import FROM, never export TO competitors. Limited parameters. Only new firmware.

Verdict: Not existential unless ALL manufacturers cooperate (economically irrational). Single-pair translations validate the market.

🚨
Scenario D: "Translation Error at a Major Show"
Swapped mix bus sends cause IEM issues at showtime

Not "if" but "when." Required: verification UX (side-by-side + acknowledgment), prominent disclaimers, E&O insurance ($1.1K/yr), rapid response plan, audit trail.

Verdict: Survivable with proper protections. The product must make users feel responsible for verification.

💰
Scenario E: "Acquisition Offer"
Console manufacturer or audio company makes an offer

Realistic interest starts at $300–500K ARR. At $100K ARR: $300K–$600K (acqui-hire). At $1M ARR: $3M–$6M. Strategic premium with bidding: $8M–$16M.

Likely acquirers: Audiotonix (DiGiCo parent), Focusrite Group (A&H parent), Audinate (Dante).

🌎
Scenario F: "Platform Play"
Expand beyond translation into full engineer toolkit

TAM expansion: Translation alone $0.5–2M → Show file management $5–10M → Full toolkit $20–50M.

Verdict: Right long-term vision, wrong short-term focus. Nail translation, build trust, then expand. Premature platform ambition kills solo founders.

13 Legal & IP Analysis

Reverse engineering for interoperability is legal in the US and EU. Get E&O insurance before launch.

JurisdictionLegal?Basis
United StatesYesDMCA Section 1201(f) — interoperability exemption
European UnionYesEU Software Directive Article 6
BrazilLikely YesFair use provisions in Copyright Law (Lei 9.610/1998), sparse case law
Legal analogy: This is the same as LibreOffice reading .docx files or GIMP reading .psd files — established, unchallenged practices. You are NOT circumventing encryption, NOT distributing original software.

Required Before Launch

E&O Insurance

$1M/occurrence, ~$1,100–$1,500/year. Covers professional negligence and software errors. Non-negotiable.

Legal Entity

Delaware LLC for legal clarity. Even if physically in Brazil, US jurisdiction simplifies IP protection.

ToS + Disclaimers

"As-is" warranty, liability cap at 12mo fees, mandatory verification acknowledgment, indemnification.

EULA Review

Check each manufacturer's license for anti-RE clauses. Consult lawyer on any restrictive terms.

16 Strategic Recommendations

The five things that matter most — and the sequence to execute them.

1. Nail translation quality before everything else.
Nothing else matters if the output can't be trusted. A single bad translation at a major show can destroy the brand.
2. A&H dLive is the #1 development priority.
Completing the "big three" (Yamaha, DiGiCo, A&H) covers the majority of touring/festival consoles. Each pair added creates exponential value (N² combinations).
3. The founder's identity as a working engineer is the greatest marketing asset.
In an industry built on trust, an engineer-founder who understands the problem viscerally will always outperform polished marketing.
4. API licensing to rental companies is the long-term revenue engine.
Individual subs build the brand; API licensing builds the revenue. One mid-tier rental company = 50 individual Pro subscribers.
5. Stay in the show file lane.
Every tool should read, write, compare, or manage show files. Diff tool = trojan horse. Version control = lock-in play.
